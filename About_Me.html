About Me

What do I find most topic interesting?

I find the topic of human behavior and psychology to be incredibly fascinating. The way people think, act, and make decisions is something I’ve always been curious about. I'm especially interested in how emotions, culture, and environmental factors influence the choices we make every day. Over time, I’ve become intrigued by how psychology plays a role not only in personal development but also in business, marketing, and even in our interactions with technology.

What I find even more interesting is how we can use psychological principles to improve communication, solve problems, and foster better relationships, both personally and professionally. For example, understanding cognitive biases can help us make better decisions, and learning about emotional intelligence can improve teamwork and leadership. As I learn more about this field, I enjoy discovering new ways to apply these insights to real-world situations, helping people grow and create a more positive impact in their environments.

What's my biggest weakness?

My biggest weakness is that I sometimes struggle with perfectionism. I hold myself to very high standards, and while this can drive me to do my best work, it also means I can become overly focused on the small details and spend more time than necessary trying to make everything perfect. This sometimes leads to me getting caught up in tasks that don’t require such a level of precision, which can delay my progress or make me feel stressed when I don’t meet my own expectations.

Over the years, I’ve learned to recognize when I’m getting caught in this cycle, and I’ve been working on finding a balance between doing high-quality work and knowing when something is “good enough.” I’ve started setting realistic goals and deadlines for myself, which helps me focus on what’s most important. I also remind myself that mistakes are part of the learning process, and I’ve been trying to embrace a more flexible approach, allowing myself to make adjustments without aiming for absolute perfection.

I believe this weakness has taught me valuable lessons about self-awareness and adaptability, and I continue to work on managing my perfectionism so that it enhances my work without holding me back.
